About

We propose to develop an integrated framework to automatically capture and manage sound clips for phonetics and phonology research in Blackfoot (an endangered Native American language spoken in Alberta, Canada, and Montana).

This project is innovative because the application of technology in Native American phonetics and phonology is underdeveloped, and it can also enhance the research methods in other languages in general.

We intend to disseminate our final project in both linguistics and computer science conferences and journals, such as the Society for the Study of the Indigenous Languages of the Americas Annual Meeting (SSILA), ACM International Conference on Multimedia, Language Documentation Conservation, and IEEE Transactions on Multimedia.

The proposed database system can be used by the Blackfoot community to create language teaching/learning materials. For example, the collected sound files can serve as pronunciation examples.

We plan to demonstrate this aspect of the project to teachers at the Blackfoot immersion school.
